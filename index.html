SEDRA PROJECT

SEDRA (Selective Entry Dynamic Risk Assessment) is a mathematical model aimed to calculating a global Safety Index for Covid-19. Most airlines, airports and Countries today require a proof vaccination plus a negative PCR test upon arrival. This virus filtering strategy is necessary but not sufficient, because of "undetected carriers". Undetected Carriers (UC), also referred to as "silent speaders", are individuals infected with the virus, who show a negative result on a PCR test. They are usually in early phase of the disease (presymptomatic, paucisymptomatic or asymptomatic) and elude the PCR test because their viral load has not reached a level sufficient to achieve a positive result.

By calculating the risk that an "undetected carrier" passenger may arrive from any given Country, this model can assess the Safety Index for each Country, which can help regulators take appropriate precautions in order to reduce the risk of importing viruses from abroad. For more info please refer to the project description.

SEDRA TABLES

A program is run every week to upload two tables to this repository:

  • A list of countries sorted alphabetically: SEDRA Report AZ
  • A list of countries sorted by Safety Index: SEDRA Report SI
  • SEDRA XLSX data file: SEDRA Report DATA

Note that only correctly reporting countries are considered. Those not reporting their Covid-19 data daily and those reporting inconsistent numbers are not included. The SEDRA models are based on the One World In Data official repository

SEDRA CHARTS

The SEDRA Charts show the evolution of the Safety Index for the reporting countries. This can be helpful to visualize patterns and forecast the behaviour.
