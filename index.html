SEDRA PROJECT Charts

SEDRA (Selective Entry Dynamic Risk Assessment) is a mathematical model aimed to calculating a global Safety Index for Covid-19. Most airlines, airports and Countries today require a proof vaccination plus a negative PCR test upon arrival. This virus filtering strategy is necessary but not sufficient, because of "undetected carriers". Undetected Carriers (UC), also referred to as "silent speaders", are individuals infected with the virus, who show a negative result on a PCR test. They are usually in early phase of the disease (pausymptomatic or asymptomatic) and elude the PCR test because their viral load has not reached a level sufficient to achieve a positive result.

By calculating the risk that an "undetected carrier" passenger may arrive from any given Country, this model can assess the Safety Index for each Country, which can help regulators take appropriate precautions in order to reduce the risk of importing viruses from abroad.
